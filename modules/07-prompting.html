Professional prompt engineers don't just "ask questions." They build structured instructions using these five pillars:

CContext
RRole
EExamples
AAudience
DDelivery

The "Before & After" Prompt Lab

Watch how adding structure changes the AI's output from "generic" to "expert."

Weak Prompt

Input: "Write a blog post about why businesses need AI in 2026."

Result: Generic, repetitive, and lacks specific business value.

Master Prompt

Input: "Act as a B2B Growth Strategist (Role). Write a 300-word LinkedIn post (Delivery) for small business owners (Audience) about GEO. Explain that AI search is replacing Google (Context). Use a bold, urgent tone and end with a call to action (Task)."

Prompting Across Different Models

OpenAI / GPT-4o

Prefers Markdown structure and direct system instructions. Responds well to "Let's think step-by-step."

Anthropic / Claude

Requires Natural Language. Explain your logic like you're speaking to a smart intern. It excels at long, complex instructions.

Google / Gemini

Best for Multimodal Context. Prompt it with images or large files for the best reasoning performance.

Advanced: Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Instead of asking for a final answer, ask the AI to show its work. This reduces hallucinations by 40% in math and logic tasks.

Prompt Hack: "Explain your reasoning for each paragraph before you write it."
