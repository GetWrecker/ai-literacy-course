Demystifying the "Next-Token Predictors" that power the modern AI revolution.

How LLMs "Read" and "Predict"

LLMs don't see words. They see Tokens—mathematical representations of text chunks. They function as highly advanced probability engines.

Large Language Models predict the next word.

When you ask a question, the model calculates the mathematical probability of every possible next token based on its training data.

Fine-Tuning vs. RAG

Fine-Tuning

Updating the actual weights of the brain. Best for teaching a specific tone or style.

RAG

Retrieval-Augmented Generation. Giving the AI an "open book" to read. Best for accuracy and private data.

Important Limitations

  • Hallucinations: The model may confidently state a "fact" that is statistically probable but factually wrong.
  • Knowledge Cutoff: Models are frozen in time based on their last training date unless connected to the web.
