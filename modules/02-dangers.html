Module 2: The Dangers of AI

With great power comes great responsibility. Understanding the risks of AI is the first step toward using it safely and ethically.

1. Algorithmic Bias

AI learns from historical data. If that data contains human prejudices—intentional or not—the AI will replicate and scale those biases.

Example: A hiring AI that favors resumes from men because it was trained on 20 years of male-dominated hiring data.

2. Deepfakes & Misinformation

Generative AI can create hyper-realistic images, videos, and audio. This makes it easier for bad actors to spread "fake news" or impersonate individuals for fraud.

  • Always verify "viral" AI content through multiple sources.
  • Check for "glitches" (extra fingers in photos, unnatural blinking in video).

3. The "Black Box" Problem

Deep learning models are often so complex that even the engineers who built them can't explain exactly why the AI made a specific decision. This lack of transparency is a major hurdle in law and medicine.

4. Data Privacy

Every prompt you give to a public AI might be used to train future versions of that model. Never upload sensitive business data, passwords, or personal medical records to public AI tools.

Risk Assessment Challenge

An AI tool suggests a medical diagnosis based on a photo. Should you trust it immediately?

No. AI should be a "Co-pilot," not the pilot. Always verify critical AI outputs with a human expert.
