PART 1: Foundations of AI Risk

AI is not inherently harmful—but becomes dangerous when it operates autonomously without oversight or scales faster than human control.

Statistical biases.

Physical safety risks.

PART 2: Ethical Risks

Bias and Discrimination

AI reflects its training data, leading to hiring bias, facial recognition inaccuracies, and algorithmic injustice in policing.

The Black Box Problem: Decisions cannot be explained, leading to a lack of transparency and an accountability crisis.

PART 3: Security Threats

  • AI-Powered Cybercrime: Automated hacking and indistinguishable phishing.
  • Deepfakes: Voice cloning and reputational damage.
  • Privacy: Mass surveillance and unauthorized data harvesting.
