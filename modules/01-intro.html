What is Artificial Intelligence?

Demystifying the technology that allows machines to think, learn, and solve problems like humans.

Learning Objectives

Define AI clearly, understand the "Data-Model-Feedback" loop, and distinguish between Narrow AI and General AI.

Understanding the Core

AI is the simulation of human intelligence by machines. It isn't just "coding"; it's building systems that improve themselves through experience.

Types of Intelligence

Narrow AI (Weak AI)

Designed for one specific task. Every AI we use today (ChatGPT, Netflix, FaceID) is Narrow AI.

General AI (Strong AI)

Human-level intelligence across all domains. This remains theoretical and has not been achieved yet.

A Brief History

1950s: The Birth
Alan Turing asks "Can machines think?" and the term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined.
1970s - 1990s: AI Winters
Periods of slow progress due to lack of data and computing power.
2010s - Present: The Boom
The rise of Big Data and GPU computing leads to Deep Learning and Generative AI.

The Responsibility Factor

AI brings immense benefits in speed and accuracy, but it also introduces Bias, Privacy Risks, and Job Displacement. Understanding AI is the first step to navigating these challenges.
